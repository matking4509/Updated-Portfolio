About Me

I am a Jack-of-All-Trades brand of Nerd. I have over 30 years of experience in various technologies and industries. When I was starting my career it was valuable asset to have a broad understanding of as many technologies as possible. So, I learned whatever I could get my hands on. I sought out information in operating systems, databases, programming languages even hardware technologies and concepts in electronics and robotics.

This broad and varied view of things, has driven my career. Growing progressively from entry-level customer support, to technology focused operations, into systems development and project management, and now into technical leadership.

Bringing this same approach to my education, I have a wide variety of unrelated Degrees and professional Certificates in Electronics, Technical Management, Culinary Arts, MBA, Scuba Diving, and as of this writing I am completing a Full Stack Bootcamp program at Georgia Tech.

Even my hobbies are eclectic; Including photography, backpacking, blacksmithing, motorcycles, cooking, scuba diving, and carpentry. Some of those I'll explore more on this site.

I have learned a lot of lessons along the way, earning me version numbers from long time friends and colleagues. Currently, I am at Mat v5.3. I try to apply lessions learned across my interests. It was surprising and interesting to find parallels in blacksmithing and cooking with project management and software development.
